# Readee sales hire plan

_Living document. Last updated: Apr 24 2026._

## What this is

A framework for when Readee should hire its first sales person and how,
written while Readee is pre-first-sale so the trigger isn't "Filip's
drowning" but "pipeline data says it's time."

The short version: we're not hiring a sales person until the pipeline
tells us we should. Scrap this doc when it does.

## Readee's sales motion today

Filip is the GTM lead. Outbound is warm — reading-specialist network,
teacher Twitter, two local-district conversations. Inbound will pick
up once SEO (`/standards/*`) and the teacher referral loop start
producing signal.

Readee has no dedicated sales rep. Everything from cold intro → pilot
close → signed DPA goes through Filip. That's fine when deal flow is
<5 active conversations; it's a bottleneck above that.

## Hire trigger (quantitative)

**Hire when at least two of these are true for two consecutive
weeks:**

- [ ] **5+ districts in active talks** (active = a real scheduled
      call in the last 30 days, not a cold email thread).
- [ ] **Pilot → paid conversion on ≥2 districts**. Converting a pilot
      means we've proven the motion; the next one is now a scaled
      problem, not a qualification problem.
- [ ] **Inbound /schools demo requests > 3/week**. If the pipeline
      fills itself, we need someone to respond fast enough.
- [ ] **Filip's calendar hits >40% sales meetings**. Past this point
      engineering velocity drops and Readee's product advantage
      starts to erode.

## What NOT to treat as a trigger

- "Other EdTech companies have sales people." They also have 5-10x
  our burn. We don't.
- "A cold email replied 'we'd love a demo.'" Single data points lie.
  We wait for a pattern of 5+ across a two-week window.
- "Filip is tired." Real but not the hire signal. Short-term fix:
  delay low-priority engineering; reprioritize toward demos.

## Compensation framework

Targeting a **full-cycle AE (Account Executive)**, not an SDR or
marketing hire. Education sales is a relationships + paperwork motion
and the cycle is too long to split roles meaningfully at Readee's
stage.

**Comp (2026 K-12 EdTech AE market, Tier 2 metros):**
- Base: $75,000 – $95,000
- OTE: $150,000 – $180,000 (with 40-50% variable)
- Equity: 0.25% – 0.75% depending on stage at hire
- Quota (year 1): $300,000 – $500,000 ARR
- Ramp: 3 months to first close, 6 months to quota

Readee-specific adjustment: quotas should be **district-ARR, not
total ARR.** A mix of 10 single-classroom B2C conversions is not the
same problem as 1 district pilot; we don't want to pay a sales
commission that penalizes district focus.

## First 6-month goals for the first hire

Month 1:
- Shadow Filip on every live call
- Own the district pilot playbook (who opens a pilot, what
  materials they need, what signals "real"
- Set up HubSpot / Attio / Salesforce (choose based on stage)

Month 2-3:
- Own 100% of inbound district conversations
- Drive outbound targeted at 50 near-Readee-fit districts
- Signed DPA count is the key metric, not demos-booked

Month 4-6:
- Close their first paid district
- Build the outbound playbook the rep can hand off / scale
- Own renewal conversation with first cohort of pilot districts

Not year-1 goals (hand back to Filip):
- Pricing negotiation on >$50k deals (Filip approves)
- Product roadmap conversations (Filip or Jennifer)
- Ed-specialist content review (Jennifer)

## Sourcing

Order of preference:
1. **Warm referral from the reading-specialist network** (Jennifer's
   contacts). Best cultural fit, best credibility.
2. **Teacher-turned-EdTech AE** (3+ years in a classroom, 2+ years
   at an EdTech company — great for district trust).
3. **Career K-12 AE** from a tier-1 competitor (Lexia, IXL, Lalilo
   alum). Fastest time-to-first-deal; lowest cultural fit.

Avoid:
- Pure SaaS AEs with no education background
- Sales managers who "want to get back in the trenches"
- Anyone who can't name the difference between the Science of
  Reading and balanced literacy in their first screen

## What to do in the meantime

While we're not hiring, focus Filip's GTM time on the cheapest-
highest-leverage moves:

1. Ship the pipeline of self-serve growth (Teacher Solo SKU, teacher
   referral loop, SEO pages, weekly parent digest — all shipped
   Apr 24 2026).
2. Close 2-3 district pilots personally — learn the motion so the
   eventual hire can model it.
3. Keep a one-page deal memo per pilot (who, what they need, where
   they got stuck). This becomes training material for the hire.
4. Build a minimal CRM — even a Notion table is fine — so the hire
   inherits history, not a blank page.

## Revisit when

- Pipeline hits the trigger above
- Readee raises a seed+ round (priced round forces a real hiring plan)
- Filip's calendar crosses the 40%-sales threshold
- A referral-quality candidate materializes unexpectedly
