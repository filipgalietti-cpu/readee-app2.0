# Claude Design brief — Friendly leaderboard (`/leaderboard`)

Paste the block below into Claude Design. Grounded in the real feature: a
kind, non-toxic ranking of how the child is doing (by carrots earned /
streak). This is for LITTLE KIDS — the whole point is warm and encouraging,
never shaming. No "you're losing," no red down-arrows, no bottom-rank

```
Redesign the LEADERBOARD page for Readee, a K-4 kids' reading app — a warm,
motivating ranking of how the child is doing (by carrots earned and streak).
This is for LITTLE KIDS, so the #1 rule is KINDNESS: it must feel encouraging
and safe, never competitive-in-a-mean-way, never shaming. It celebrates effort
and shows the child they belong and are climbing.

WHO IT'S FOR
One child, 5-10 yrs, on a laptop/tablet. Low reading ability — lean on the
bunny, position art, color, and simple "you're doing great / moving up" cues
over text. One kid per account (never show child pickers).

THE KIND FRAMING (this is the whole point — get it right)
- Encouraging, not competitive. Frame everything as "look how far you've come"
  and "you're moving up," NOT "you're behind" or "you're losing."
- NEVER show the child as last / at the bottom, never a "worst" callout, no red
  down-arrows, no shaming language. If the child is low, frame it as "just
  getting started — keep going!" and show the encouraging path upward.
- Other kids appear friendly and anonymized (cute bunny avatars + friendly
  display names/initials), never in a way that singles anyone out negatively.
  Everyone on the board is "doing great."
- Optional gentle time framing (This Week) so a slow start always resets — a
  fresh, hopeful week, no permanent losers.

SCREEN ANATOMY
- The kid's OWN CARD is the hero — pulled out and highlighted: their Readee
  BUNNY (in its equipped outfit), their name, their rank shown warmly ("You're
  #4 — awesome!"), carrots earned, and streak. A little "moving up!" flourish
  if they climbed.
- NEARBY RANKS: show a small window of the kids just above and just below (so
  the next spot always feels reachable — "just 12 carrots to pass!"), rather
  than an overwhelming full list. Each row: a friendly bunny avatar, name,
  carrots/streak, position. The kid's own row stays visually distinct.
- A gentle "top climbers" celebration for the very top few (a little podium /
  crown of encouragement) — celebrating them WITHOUT making everyone else feel
  small. Keep it warm, not a trophy-hoarding flex.
- A clear, kind motivation line + CTA: "Read a story to climb!" linking back to
  practice/stories to earn more carrots.

REWARD + MOTION
- Springy row entrances, the kid's card popping in proudly, carrots flying to
  the counter, a happy bunny bob, a gentle "you moved up!" celebration when
  rank improves. Warm sparkle, never harsh. Nothing that feels like a loss.

VISUAL SYSTEM (match the redesigned app — not a rebrand)
- Fonts: Baloo 2 display, Nunito body.
- Palette: indigo/violet (#4338ca → #7c3aed), gold carrots, watercolor/sky
  pastel background. Rounded chunky cards, one consistent shadow depth,
  Framer-Motion-style springy micro-motion.
- Bright, warm, encouraging, credibly educational — a supportive cheer squad,
  not a competitive scoreboard.

HARD CONSTRAINTS
- NO native emoji (Lucide-style icons / illustrated art only).
- NO plain black text on colored surfaces.
- Carrots (not gems/coins); the Readee bunny (not a new mascot) — every kid is
  a bunny avatar.
- B2C kid surface — one kid per account, no teacher/classroom anything (this is
  NOT a class ranking — it's a friendly global/anonymized board).
- Kindness constraint: no shaming, no "last place," no red negative indicators,
  no losers — every child is encouraged.
- Desktop AND tablet layouts. The kid's hero card and nearby rows stay big and
  readable on both.

DELIVERABLE
A polished, responsive friendly leaderboard: the kid's highlighted hero card
with their bunny/rank/carrots/streak, a nearby-ranks window (just above/below,
with a "X to pass!" nudge), a warm top-climbers celebration, and a kind
"earn more carrots" CTA. Show 2 states: a child ranked in the middle
("moving up!"), and a brand-new child near the start framed encouragingly as
"just getting started — keep going!" (never as last place).
```
